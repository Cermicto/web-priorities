Developer Workshops: All Skill Level Welcome

Aside from performing work for clients I enjoy helping would be developers and experienced developers alike with their current development targets.

The workshop sessions encourage those who have never written a line of code in their life to dive in and see how powerful some easy to learn skills can allow them to create usefully functional web site components. There is no set of steps for lessons planned out as I work right where learners are on just what learners want to learn. I throw in all sorts of related stuff along the way so learners can jump at things related to just what they want and get more benefit from time spent together.

Workshops with experienced developers focus on their personal work sometimes and also on advanced concepts they aren't getting paid to learn. I assist with people getting paid to do the things I help them along with and find great benefit to both of us knowing they are accomplishing paid tasks. Sometimes while learning web programming there are just blind spots with no obvious way of tackling what needs to be done and that's when my over a decade of work experience comes in. Nearly anything imaginable is doable as far as I'm concerned. Many times the largest blocks in the way are from frameworks or particular steps in development environments anyway. Nothing that can't be overcame.

Taking Things to the Big Leagues

Remote workdays tend to start off easy before working their way into experience-based necessary skill usage. Not all developers have the same experience and this can lead to the employers being let down when a stumbling block is just too much to take on. With assisted starts developers with actual experience can choose to aid or take over entirely when deadlines are being presented. Both developers gain start and continuing experience if they choose. There is not competition so much as interest-based job aquisition. Paying one developer and allowing the second to learn the job even allows for reversing a take over when skills are aquired. This lets the remote employer trust a developer-as-a-team with only the comfortableness with the work as a skill to aquire.
